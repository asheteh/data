How to find sargam & notation of any songs :

To be able to recognize the notes in a song, you must first know that there are 12 notes various combinations of which essentially comprise any song in the world. Having asked this question, I believe you to be a student of music and expect you to have atleast a basic knowledge of music theory. With that in hand, I want to assure you that within a time of a month or two, you can start feeling more confident, but, if and only if you follow the instructions below. The things that I am going to write down are not secret. What it takes to reach your goal is nothing but your determination, practice and learning to reveal that secret.

Let's concentrate now.
The straight forward answer to your question is - ear training. Before we reach the "how to" part, you should be knowing that there are 7 distinct swaras. Two of them remain fixed, whereas the rest five occur in the form of two variants. I give below a brief portrayal of how they occur in an octave.

Out of the seven, Sa and Pa have fixed positions; Re, Ga, Dha and Ni can be both shuddh (natural) and komal (flat); Ma can be shuddh and tivra (sharp). In total they become 12. These are referred to as chal swar (volatile notes) and achal swar (fixed notes). Clearly, Sa and Pa sit in the latter.

Now, to begin with one must know that the different notes are situated at different distances from the root note. To understand this, let's take example of a harmonium. Take the first black key (pehli kaali/ kaali ek, C# note) as the root note. Starting from there,
the 1st, 3rd, 5th, 6th, 8th, 10th and 12th notes will give you all the shuddh swaras.
the 2nd, 4th, 9th and 11th will be the komal swaras.
the 7th note will give tivra ma.

Let's try and understand how one can teach his ears to identify the notes without playing an instrument.
For this, first of all you need a tanpura .
When you are ready with your tanpura playing by your side, listen to it carefully. Let your ears submerge into the sound. Let yourself interlise the ambience. Do this for few minutes. Focus on Sa. Now, sing Sa gently in full volume with mouth easily wide open; no stress on your body. Try to inhale from your nose and exhale from your mouth. This will help you build stamina and give you a better voice texture. Do it for some 15 minutes (atleast) before heading further. After this you will be practicing some alankars. There can be innumerable alankars. To start, do this:

Don't haste when you practice. Think every time you hit a note. Try to judge whether you are producing it right. You can practice any alankar you want. Practicing alankars will train your ears to recognize the sound as musical notes. Gradually you will develop a sense to do what your question asks. While practising, please do not use a harmonium. Taking help from harmonium will only bring dispare. As a must, you should go to your music teacher for verifying if what you are doing is correct. If you honestly spend atleast an hour each morning and evening everyday practising, you will surely progress extensively very soon. Moreover, practising bandishes and ragas will also help a great deal, but, only alongwith the above exercise.

In nutshell, the above explanation is about developing an understanding of the distance of notes from the root note and the places where they situate. Any song is created using only these 12 notes. Doing alankars will make you more familiar with them. You can use all types of swaras interchangeably to understand things more deeply. Try using the 10 thaats for this purpose.

Perfection comes with time. Don't dishearten yourself. It will take time to become a part of your behaviour. Rushing will bring nothing. Stay calm and keep practicing.
